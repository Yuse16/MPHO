## 2. Brand ownership plan

Before launch:

- Confirm legal owner.
- Search MPHO.
- search HADIA.
- search MPHORA.
- review confusingly similar marks.
- review relevant classes.
- review domain.
- review social handles.
- file applications when approved.
- document logo author and assignment.
- document design and code ownership.
- record renewal deadlines.

Do not place `®` unless registration permits it.

Use `™` only with counsel-approved brand practice.

---

## 3. Brand hierarchy

```text
MPHO — master platform
HADIA — AI gift assistant
MPHORA — fast-delivery mode
MPHO Aliados — partner product
Punto MPHO — physical partner role
```

The agreement and design system must define who may use each name.

---

## 4. Partner brand license

Partner may receive a limited license to use:

- Punto MPHO badge.
- Approved window sign.
- MPHO Aliados material.
- campaign content.

License should be:

- Non-exclusive.
- non-transferable.
- revocable.
- limited to agreement.
- subject to brand guide.
- no domain or social handle registration.
- no logo modification.
- no implication of ownership.
- removed after termination.

---

## 5. Partner content license

Partner must confirm rights to provide:

- Product photos.
- descriptions.
- logos.
- brand names.
- videos.
- catalog data.
- promotions.

The license to MPHO should cover only required use:

- Display.
- crop/resize.
- distribute within platform.
- promote the listing if authorized.
- preserve historical order evidence.

MPHO should not claim ownership of partner content.

---

## 6. Customer content

Customer content may include:

- Message.
- photo.
- audio.
- video.
- QR destination.
- name.
- design.

Customer should grant a limited license to:

- Store.
- process.
- share with assigned partner.
- create the requested personalization.
- provide support.
- secure and investigate abuse.

No advertising use without separate permission.

---

## 7. Customer-content restrictions

Block or review content that:

- Is illegal.
- threatens.
- harasses.
- sexualizes minors.
- exposes private data.
- infringes copyright or trademark.
- impersonates.
- contains malware.
- directs to phishing.
- requests extremist or prohibited material.
- creates a safety risk.

A moderation decision should be appealable when practical.

---

## 8. QR links

QR destinations must:

- Use HTTPS where possible.
- avoid malware.
- avoid phishing.
- avoid unauthorized tracking.
- use a safe MPHO redirect or hosted page.
- expire or be revocable when appropriate.
- respect privacy.
- not expose storage URLs.
- not redirect to private networks.
- not permit script execution.

Customer-provided external URLs require validation.

---

## 9. AI-generated images

AI-generated visuals may be used for:

- Concept design.
- category illustration.
- marketing creative.
- fictional demonstration.

They must not be used to misrepresent:

- Exact product.
- exact store.
- real partnership.
- product condition.
- ingredients.
- recipient result.
- official endorsement.

When a generated image is illustrative, label it when a customer could reasonably mistake it for the exact product.

---

## 10. AI-generated text

AI-generated descriptions must be reviewed for:

- False facts.
- unverified material.
- prohibited claims.
- missing restrictions.
- unsafe advice.
- IP misuse.
- fake reviews.
- invented local origin.
- invented certification.

AI does not create legal clearance.

---

## 11. Third-party brands

Use third-party marks only when:

- Necessary and lawful.
- Accurate.
- not confusing.
- not implying affiliation.
- not larger or more prominent than appropriate.
- consistent with marketplace/source disclosure.
- not used in a domain or product name without permission.

---

## 12. Character and fandom products

Products using:

- Disney.
- anime.
- sports teams.
- musicians.
- films.
- games.
- celebrities.
- logos.

Require evidence of lawful merchandise or a legitimate source.

Do not accept “inspired” as automatic clearance.

---

## 13. Counterfeit policy

Prohibited:

- Replica presented as original.
- unauthorized logo product.
- pirated media.
- fake certificate.
- copied design.
- altered brand label.
- suspicious external listing.

Flow:

```text
detect
→ unpublish
→ preserve evidence
→ notify partner
→ investigate
→ refund/return affected order
→ restrict or terminate partner
→ document
```

---

## 14. Copyright and software

The repository should document:

- Employee or contractor assignment.
- open-source licenses.
- third-party packages.
- fonts.
- icons.
- images.
- music.
- stock assets.
- AI tool terms.

No asset should enter production without a known license source.

---

## 15. Open-source compliance

Maintain:

- Dependency list.
- License.
- attribution.
- source-offer obligations when applicable.
- modification notice.
- prohibited or incompatible license review.
- SBOM.
- update.

Do not copy code from an AI answer without license and security review.

---

## 16. Photography

For real local stores:

- Written permission.
- correct store name.
- scope of use.
- expiration or termination.
- no private people without permission.
- no address exposure beyond public business information.
- no fake rating.
- no fake “Aliado MPHO” status.

For people:

- model release when required.
- guardian authorization for minors.
- no deceptive context.
- no sensitive use.

---

## 17. Reviews and testimonials

Requirements:

- Real experience.
- permission.
- no fabricated review.
- disclose incentive.
- do not edit meaning.
- rating source.
- moderation.
- fraud detection.
- removal process.

AI may summarize reviews only if the summary is faithful and source rights permit.

---

## 18. Cultural and indigenous content

Products or branding using:

- Sarape motifs.
- indigenous designs.
- community names.
- traditional cultural expressions.
- sacred symbols.

Require heightened review.

Do not assume traditional appearance means public-domain commercial use without obligations.

Use respectful attribution and authorized sourcing.

---

## 19. Takedown process

Accept claims regarding:

- Copyright.
- trademark.
- counterfeit.
- privacy.
- unauthorized photo.
- impersonation.
- harmful content.

Record:

```text
claimant
contact
right
content
URL/listing
basis
evidence
date
temporary action
partner response
decision
appeal
repeat actor
```

Do not expose claimant data unnecessarily.

---

## 20. Repeat infringer or repeat violator

Potential actions:

- Warning.
- listing removal.
- capability restriction.
- earnings hold related to disputed transaction.
- account suspension.
- termination.
- evidence preservation.

The policy must avoid arbitrary seizure of unrelated earnings.

---

- impersonation monitoring.

- renewal.

- no abandoned subdomain.

## 23. Public content notice skeleton

> Las imágenes y descripciones de productos deben representar de forma fiel el artículo ofrecido. Las imágenes ilustrativas o generadas con inteligencia artificial se identificarán cuando puedan confundirse con el producto exacto. Las marcas de terceros pertenecen a sus titulares y su mención no implica afiliación. MPHO prohíbe productos falsificados, piratas o que infrinjan derechos de terceros.
