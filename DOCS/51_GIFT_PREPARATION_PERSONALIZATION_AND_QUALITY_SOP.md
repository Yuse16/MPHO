# 51_GIFT_PREPARATION_PERSONALIZATION_AND_QUALITY_SOP.md

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines how a Punto MPHO prepares, wraps, personalizes, verifies, documents, seals, and releases a gift.

The objective is to make the delivered gift match the exact authorized order while protecting quality, privacy, timing, and evidence.

---

## 2. Preparation may begin only when

- Partner accepted the order.
- Product is physically available.
- External package passed inspection when applicable.
- Personalization content is approved.
- Substitutions are resolved.
- Preparation task is active.
- No blocking incident exists.
- Required materials are available.

Do not begin irreversible work before the required customer approval.

---

## 3. Preparation workspace

The workspace must be:

- Clean.
- Dry.
- Organized.
- Restricted from customers.
- Free from unrelated products.
- Suitable for the product category.
- Safe for files and personal information.
- Equipped for evidence capture.

Food, flowers, cosmetics, fragile items, and electronics may require separate handling rules.

---

## 4. Order verification before opening task

The operator confirms:

```text
order number
product
variant
quantity
wrapping type
personalization
message
recipient mode
delivery date
deadline
special handling
required evidence
```

The operator must not prepare from a screenshot or WhatsApp message alone.

---

## 5. Product verification

Before wrapping:

- Match product to order.
- Match variant.
- Match quantity.
- Check visible condition.
- Check expiration when applicable.
- Check accessories.
- Check approved substitution.
- Remove external price tags only when authorized and appropriate.
- Preserve warranty information.

If there is a mismatch, stop and open an incident.

---

## 6. Personalization approval

For text, images, audio, video, engraving, printing, or QR:

- Show a preview when possible.
- Record approved version.
- Record approval time.
- Lock approved content.
- Record any customer correction.
- Do not rely on memory or informal chat.

Any material change requires new approval.

---

## 7. Content safety review

Review for:

- Threats.
- Harassment.
- Illegal content.
- Phishing link.
- Malware.
- Private data exposure.
- Copyright or trademark concerns.
- Sexual content involving minors.
- Recipient safety risk.

Uncertain or dangerous content is escalated.

---

## 8. Wrapping standards

Wrapping should be:

- Appropriate to product size.
- Protective.
- Clean.
- Free of visible damage.
- Consistent with selected tier.
- Secure for transport.
- Free of unauthorized partner advertising.

Document approved materials and substitutions.

---

## 9. Fragile items

For fragile gifts:

- Add internal cushioning.
- Prevent movement.
- Protect corners.
- Mark handling needs.
- Use appropriate outer packaging.
- Avoid decorative choices that reduce safety.
- Inform courier through the official delivery record.

---

## 10. Perishable items

For perishables:

- Confirm freshness.
- Confirm safe handling.
- Confirm delivery timing.
- Record preparation time.
- Avoid early sealing that harms quality.
- Include storage or consumption information when required.
- Escalate delivery delay quickly.

---

## 11. Gift message

The message must match the approved text.

Check:

- Recipient name.
- Sender name or anonymity.
- Spelling.
- Date.
- Emoji or symbols.
- Language.
- Privacy.

Do not add staff comments or marketing.

---

## 12. QR media

Before printing or attaching:

- Use approved MPHO link or validated destination.
- Test QR.
- Confirm correct order.
- Confirm access permissions.
- Confirm expiration or retention.
- Confirm no private storage URL is exposed.
- Confirm destination works on mobile.

A QR should not point directly to a dangerous or unverified URL.

---

## 13. Substitution procedure

A substitution requires:

- Original item unavailable.
- Proposed replacement documented.
- Price difference documented.
- Quality and feature difference explained.
- Customer approval recorded.
- Quote or refund difference processed.
- Order snapshot updated.

No silent substitution.

---

## 14. Preparation checklist

Suggested task sequence:

```text
verify product
→ verify personalization
→ prepare materials
→ assemble
→ quality check
→ evidence
→ seal
→ label
→ mark ready
```

The same person may perform and check low-risk orders during MVP, but higher-risk orders should use a second checker.

---

## 15. Quality-control review

Verify:

- Correct product.
- Correct variant.
- Correct quantity.
- Correct message.
- Correct wrapping.
- Correct personalization.
- No price tag visible when it should be removed.
- No damage.
- No foreign object.
- No unauthorized advertising.
- Package can survive delivery.
- Label matches order.
- Recipient details are not visible unnecessarily.

---

## 16. Evidence requirements

Evidence may include:

- Product before wrapping.
- Personalization close-up.
- Final gift.
- Sealed package.
- Order identifier.
- Required quality detail.

Evidence must not reveal:

- Full payment data.
- Another order.
- Unrelated customer details.
- Staff personal content.
- Store customer faces without need.

---

## 17. Evidence fraud prevention

Controls:

- File hash.
- Timestamp.
- Order link.
- Duplicate-image detection.
- Dynamic order challenge for selected risk.
- Random review.
- No reuse from catalog photo.
- No excessive editing.

---

## 18. Packaging label

The external delivery label should include only necessary information:

- Order reference.
- Courier reference.
- Handling mark.
- Recipient details required for delivery.

Avoid displaying gift content or private message.

---

## 19. Mark ready

Partner may mark ready only when:

- Checklist complete.
- Evidence uploaded.
- Package sealed.
- No incident.
- Pickup location confirmed.
- Deadline remains viable.

Marking ready creates an auditable event.

---

## 20. Late preparation

When delay is likely:

1. Update estimated completion.
2. Select reason.
3. Notify operations.
4. Preserve customer promise.
5. Determine whether courier should be delayed.
6. Determine whether customer needs options.
7. Open incident when material.

Do not mark ready to avoid a deadline.

---

## 21. Preparation error

Examples:

- Wrong name.
- Wrong color.
- Damaged wrapping.
- Missing add-on.
- Broken product.
- Bad print.
- QR failure.

Procedure:

```text
stop
→ photograph
→ preserve materials
→ report
→ estimate correction
→ obtain approval if timing changes
→ correct or refund
```

---

## 22. Courier handoff readiness

Before handoff:

- Courier verified.
- Correct order selected.
- Package count confirmed.
- Condition recorded.
- Handling instruction visible.
- Recipient mode understood.
- Handoff evidence captured.

---

## 23. Required metrics

- Preparation time.
- Rework rate.
- personalization error.
- missing item.
- evidence rejection.
- late ready.
- damage before pickup.
- customer quality complaint.

---

## 24. Definition of done

A gift is prepared correctly when:

- It matches the authorized order.
- The personalization matches the approved version.
- The gift is safe for delivery.
- Evidence is complete.
- The package is sealed and correctly labeled.
- No unresolved quality issue exists.
