# 02_PRODUCT_VISION.md

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the long-term product vision for MPHO.

It explains:

- What MPHO is trying to become.
- Which problem it must solve better than existing alternatives.
- Which users it serves.
- Which principles guide product decisions.
- Which outcomes define success.
- Which future opportunities belong to the vision but not necessarily to the MVP.

This document is strategic. It must guide roadmap, design, operations, partnerships, automation, and technical architecture.

---

## 2. Vision statement

MPHO aims to become the easiest and most trusted way to find, prepare, personalize, and deliver a meaningful gift through a distributed network of local partners.

The long-term experience should feel like:

```text
I need to send something meaningful
→ MPHO helps me decide
→ MPHO finds the best available option
→ a nearby partner prepares it
→ MPHO coordinates delivery
→ I receive clear confirmation
```

The customer should not have to coordinate several stores, providers, wrappers, and couriers independently.

---

## 3. Mission

MPHO exists to make gifting simple, reliable, local, and emotionally meaningful.

The mission is to coordinate the complete gifting process while creating new revenue opportunities for local businesses.

MPHO must combine:

- Product discovery.
- Local commerce.
- External-product fulfillment.
- Gift preparation.
- Personalization.
- Payment.
- Local delivery.
- Tracking.
- Exception management.

---

## 4. Product promise

The core promise is:

> MPHO helps you choose, prepare, and send the right gift without making you coordinate every operational step.

This promise depends on three capabilities:

### HADIA

Helps the customer decide what to give.

### MPHORA

Finds operationally eligible gifts that can be delivered quickly.

### MPHO Aliados

Allows local partners to accept, prepare, document, and dispatch orders.

## 5. The future MPHO experience

A mature MPHO experience should allow a customer to:

1. Explain the occasion naturally.
2. Indicate recipient, budget, location, and required date.
3. Receive curated suggestions from HADIA.
4. Choose between local, MPHORA, and by-order products.
5. Personalize the gift.
6. Know the complete price before payment.
7. Pay securely.
8. Follow preparation and delivery.
9. Receive evidence of completion.
10. Save the occasion for future reminders.
11. Repeat the purchase with less effort next time.

The mature partner experience should allow a business to:

1. Join the network.
2. Define capabilities and zones.
3. Publish approved products.
4. Receive structured orders.
5. Accept or reject within a clear time window.
6. Confirm stock or package receipt.
7. Prepare using clear instructions.
8. Upload evidence.
9. Dispatch to a courier.
10. View earnings and payouts.
11. Improve visibility through reliable performance.

## 6. Problem definition

### 6.1 Customer-side fragmentation

Customers frequently need to coordinate:

- A product source.
- Availability.
- Payment.
- Wrapping.
- Personalization.
- Address validation.
- Delivery.
- Tracking.
- Proof of receipt.

This creates friction, uncertainty, and failed deliveries.

### 6.2 Local-commerce limitations

Many local gift businesses have valuable products and preparation skills but lack:

- Ecommerce.
- Automation.
- Paid traffic.
- Structured order intake.
- Digital payment integration.
- Delivery coordination.
- Operational tracking.
- Partner analytics.
- Tools for receiving third-party packages.

### 6.3 External-product gap

A customer may find a suitable item on a marketplace but still needs:

- A local receiving point.
- Inspection.
- Wrapping.
- Personalization.
- Last-mile delivery.

MPHO creates the missing operational layer between an external product and the final recipient.

## 8. Target users

### 8.1 Primary customer

A person who wants to send a gift but values convenience, certainty, and emotional relevance.

Possible situations:

- Lives far from the recipient.
- Needs same-day or scheduled delivery.
- Does not know what to choose.
- Wants a personalized gift.
- Found a product online but needs local preparation.
- Wants to avoid coordinating multiple vendors.

### 8.2 Local partner

A local business that wants more sales or additional service revenue.

Examples:

- Florists.
- Gift stores.
- Balloon stores.
- Chocolate shops.
- Bakeries.
- Personalized-product businesses.
- Wrapping specialists.
- Convenience points capable of receiving and preparing packages.

### 8.3 MPHO operator

A person who handles exceptions, partner support, catalog quality, and operational control.

### 8.4 Recipient

The person receiving the gift.

The recipient experience matters even when the recipient does not have an MPHO account.

## 9. Jobs to be done

### Customer job

> Help me send an appropriate gift to someone, on time, without having to coordinate every step myself.

### Partner job

> Help me earn from my products, preparation skills, location, and delivery capacity without requiring me to build my own ecommerce and automation system.

## 10. Product principles

### 10.1 Truth before conversion

Do not increase conversion through false urgency, fake stock, or unverified delivery promises.

### 10.2 Reliability before catalog size

A smaller reliable catalog is better than a large inaccurate catalog.

### 10.3 Local density before geographic expansion

Build strong partner coverage in one area before opening many weak areas.

### 10.4 One coordinated experience

The customer should experience one coherent service even when several operational actors are involved.

### 10.5 Human meaning, automated operations

The experience should feel personal while repetitive coordination is automated.

### 10.6 Exceptions deserve visibility

Healthy orders should progress quietly. Problems should become visible quickly.

### 10.7 Mobile-first operation

Customers, partners, and couriers are expected to use mobile devices frequently.

### 10.8 Explainable AI

HADIA should explain why an option fits the customer and must not invent availability.

### 10.9 Clear economics

Every participant should understand what they pay, earn, or are responsible for.

### 10.10 Evidence creates trust

Critical stages should be supported by timestamps, status history, and evidence where appropriate.

## 11. Product pillars

### Pillar 1: Discovery

Customers can search directly or ask HADIA.

### Pillar 2: Availability

The platform understands whether a product is:

- Local.
- MPHORA eligible.
- By order.
- Temporarily unavailable.

### Pillar 3: Preparation

Partners receive structured instructions and complete preparation tasks.

### Pillar 4: Coordination

MPHO connects payment, partner, external provider, and courier.

### Pillar 5: Trust

The customer receives status updates, clear pricing, evidence, and support.

### Pillar 6: Partner economics

Partners see earnings, services performed, and payout status.

### Pillar 7: Recurrence

MPHO can later help customers remember future occasions and repeat successful gifting behavior.

## 13. North-star outcome

The main long-term outcome is:

> A customer can successfully send an appropriate gift through MPHO with minimal manual coordination and reliable delivery confirmation.

Potential supporting metrics:

- Completed orders.
- On-time delivery rate.
- Repeat purchase rate.
- Partner acceptance rate.
- Order exception rate.
- Customer satisfaction.
- Partner retention.
- Average time from payment to partner acceptance.
- Percentage of orders progressing without MPHO operator intervention.

No numerical targets should be established until real operational data exists.

## 14. Product horizons

### Horizon 1: Validate the workflow

Focus:

- One city.
- Few partners.
- Curated products.
- Manual or assisted assignment.
- Basic payments.
- Partner PWA.
- Structured order states.
- Manual courier coordination when needed.
- WhatsApp notifications.
- Earnings records.

Goal:

Prove that the full order lifecycle works.

### Horizon 2: Improve automation and density

Focus:

- More partners by zone.
- Automatic assignment rules.
- Better stock synchronization.
- Automated notifications.
- Multiple delivery options.
- Partner scoring.
- Stronger exception workflows.
- HADIA grounded in catalog data.
- MPHORA eligibility engine.

Goal:

Reduce human intervention while improving reliability.

### Horizon 3: Expand the network

Focus:

- Additional cities.
- Corporate gifting.
- Reminder engine.
- Partner self-service onboarding with review.
- Advanced payouts.
- Supplier integrations.
- Multi-city operations.
- Personalization marketplace.
- Repeat-order intelligence.

Goal:

Become a scalable gifting network without losing operational trust.

## 16. Anti-vision

MPHO must not become:

- A generic marketplace with no operational control.
- A catalog that copies products without validating availability.
- A delivery promise engine based on guesses.
- A chatbot that recommends fictional products.
- A manual WhatsApp operation disguised as software.
- A platform that hides fees.
- A system that treats partners as interchangeable without quality controls.
- A national platform before local operations work.
- A complex fintech product unrelated to the gifting experience.
- A warehouse-heavy retailer unless a future strategic decision explicitly changes the model.

## 18. Decision filter

When evaluating a new feature, ask:

1. Does it improve successful gift delivery?
2. Does it reduce customer coordination?
3. Does it improve partner execution?
4. Does it increase reliability?
5. Does it protect money and data?
6. Can it be supported operationally?
7. Does it belong to the current phase?
8. Is there real evidence of demand?
9. Does it create unnecessary complexity?
10. Can the value be measured?

A feature should not be prioritized only because it appears modern or uses AI.

## 19. Validation strategy

The vision must be validated through real operations.

Early validation should include:

- Interviews with local gift businesses.
- Interviews with potential customers.
- Catalog pilots.
- Real delivery quotes.
- Test orders.
- External-package receiving tests.
- Wrapping-time measurement.
- Partner earnings feedback.
- Customer willingness-to-pay tests.
- Exception tracking.

Hypotheses must be labeled as hypotheses until validated.

## 20. Vision summary

MPHO should become a trusted gifting network that combines local products, external products, AI-assisted discovery, distributed preparation, and local delivery.

HADIA helps choose.

MPHORA identifies fast eligible options.

MPHO Aliados turns local businesses into reliable operational nodes.

The product succeeds when the customer can send something meaningful without managing every operational detail.
