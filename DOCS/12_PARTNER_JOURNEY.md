# 12_PARTNER_JOURNEY.md

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the end-to-end journey of a partner in MPHO.

It covers:

- Discovery and recruitment.
- Application.
- Review.
- Activation.
- Catalog setup.
- Order acceptance.
- External-package receipt.
- Preparation.
- Evidence.
- Courier handoff.
- Earnings.
- Payouts.
- Performance.
- Pausing.
- Suspension.
- Offboarding.

The partner-facing application is MPHO Aliados.

---

## 2. Partner objective

The partner's job is:

> Help me receive additional sales and paid preparation work through clear orders, predictable responsibilities, and transparent earnings.

The journey should reduce:

- Long WhatsApp negotiations.
- Unclear instructions.
- Unclear pricing.
- Unclear payment status.
- Repeated customer questions.
- Confusion about delivery.
- Confusion about responsibility.

---

## 3. Partner journey overview

```text
learn about MPHO
→ apply
→ submit business and capability information
→ review
→ training
→ test order
→ activation
→ configure catalog and availability
→ receive offers
→ accept and fulfill
→ upload evidence
→ hand off
→ view earnings
→ receive payout
→ improve performance
```

---

## 4. Stage 1: Partner discovery

Potential acquisition channels:

- Direct local visit.
- WhatsApp outreach.
- Referral.
- Local business group.
- Social media.
- Partner event.
- Existing partner recommendation.
- Inbound application.

The partner message should explain:

- What MPHO is.
- What work may be requested.
- How the partner may earn.
- What technology is required.
- What evidence is required.
- How payouts work.
- That order volume is not guaranteed.

---

## 5. Stage 2: Initial interest

The interested business should be able to review:

- Partner benefits.
- Partner responsibilities.
- Eligible categories.
- Required capabilities.
- Example order flow.
- Example earnings structure.
- Payment schedule concept.
- Data and privacy expectations.
- Application requirements.

The application should not hide operational workload.

---

## 6. Stage 3: Application

Required information may include:

- Business name.
- Responsible person.
- Phone.
- Email.
- Address.
- City.
- Zone.
- Operating hours.
- Business category.
- Product categories.
- Capabilities.
- Photos.
- Package-receiving capacity.
- Storage capacity.
- Delivery capability.
- Payment information.
- Tax information when required.
- Agreement acceptance.

Application state:

```text
draft
→ submitted
→ under_review
```

---

## 7. Stage 4: Review

MPHO may review:

- Identity.
- Location.
- Business relevance.
- Product quality.
- Wrapping quality.
- Operating schedule.
- Package security.
- Technology readiness.
- Zone demand.
- Delivery capability.
- Risk category.

Possible results:

- Information required.
- Approved.
- Approved with restrictions.
- Rejected.
- Waitlisted.

---

## 8. Stage 5: Capability approval

Capabilities are approved independently.

Example:

```text
sell_local_products: approved
receive_external_packages: approved
wrap_basic: approved
wrap_premium: pending
deliver_locally: rejected
```

A partner may become active with only some capabilities.

---

## 9. Stage 6: Agreement

Before activation, the partner must accept:

- Commercial terms.
- Operational rules.
- Privacy obligations.
- Evidence requirements.
- Payout process.
- Incident rules.
- Suspension rules.
- Brand-use rules.
- Product responsibilities.
- Package responsibilities.

Agreement version must be stored.

---

## 10. Stage 7: Training

Training may include:

- Sign in.
- New-order notifications.
- Accepting.
- Rejecting.
- Stock confirmation.
- Package receipt.
- Damage report.
- Preparation.
- Evidence.
- Ready status.
- Courier handoff.
- Earnings.
- Payouts.
- Incidents.
- Data privacy.

Training status should be recorded.

---

## 11. Stage 8: Test order

The partner may complete a test order.

Test flow:

```text
receive notification
→ open order
→ accept
→ confirm stock or receipt
→ prepare
→ upload evidence
→ mark ready
→ confirm handoff
```

MPHO evaluates:

- Response time.
- Instruction understanding.
- Evidence quality.
- Preparation quality.
- Status accuracy.
- App usability.

---

## 12. Stage 9: Activation

Activation requires:

- Approved status.
- Required capabilities.
- Agreement.
- Training.
- Payment method.
- Active schedule.
- At least one valid service or product.
- No blocking review.

Once active, the partner may receive real offers.

---

## 13. Stage 10: Profile setup

The partner configures:

- Public name.
- Operating hours.
- Holiday hours.
- Contact information.
- Available capabilities.
- Temporary capacity.
- Receiving schedule.
- Delivery zones when applicable.
- Preparation times.
- Payout information.

Some fields require MPHO approval.

---

## 14. Stage 11: Catalog setup

The partner may submit:

- Product name.
- Product description.
- Images.
- Variants.
- Supply or retail price.
- Stock.
- Preparation time.
- Personalization.
- Add-ons.
- Substitution options.
- Category.

Catalog workflow:

```text
draft
→ submitted
→ under_review
→ approved
→ published
```

MPHO may standardize product presentation.

---

## 15. Stage 12: Availability

The partner should manage:

- Open or closed.
- Temporary pause.
- Order capacity.
- Product availability.
- Service availability.
- Package-receiving availability.
- Delivery availability.
- Holiday schedule.

Availability may expire automatically.

---

## 16. Stage 13: New order offer

The partner receives:

- Notification.
- Order number.
- Product or service.
- Required work.
- Timing.
- Delivery window.
- Customer-approved instructions.
- Expected earning.
- Accept-by deadline.

The partner should not need to ask basic questions outside the platform.

---

## 17. Stage 14: Offer review

The partner checks:

- Product availability.
- Materials.
- Staff capacity.
- Deadline.
- Personalization.
- Receiving capability.
- Special handling.
- Earnings.
- Delivery handoff.

Available actions:

- Accept.
- Reject.
- Report stock issue.
- Request clarification.
- Report unsupported requirement.

---

## 18. Stage 15: Acceptance

Acceptance creates responsibility.

The partner confirms:

- Work is understood.
- Timing is feasible.
- Capacity exists.
- Required materials exist.
- Status updates will be completed.
- Evidence will be uploaded.
- Data will remain private.

The partner should see the next required action immediately.

---

## 19. Stage 16A: Local-product fulfillment

Flow:

```text
offer accepted
→ stock confirmed
→ product reserved
→ preparation scheduled
→ preparation started
→ evidence uploaded
→ marked ready
```

The partner must report stock mismatch immediately.

---

## 20. Stage 16B: External-product fulfillment

Flow:

```text
offer accepted
→ partner confirms receiving availability
→ package expected
→ package arrives
→ receipt recorded
→ condition inspected
→ issue reported or product approved
→ preparation starts
→ evidence uploaded
→ marked ready
```

The partner should see expected:

- Provider.
- Tracking reference when available.
- Package description.
- Expected date.
- Order reference.
- Receiving instructions.

---

## 21. Stage 17: Receipt and inspection

The partner records:

- Received timestamp.
- Person who received.
- Package condition.
- Product match.
- Quantity.
- Visible damage.
- Missing components.
- Photos.

Possible outcomes:

```text
received_ok
received_damaged
received_incomplete
received_wrong_item
not_received
```

Only `received_ok` should continue automatically.

---

## 22. Stage 18: Issue reporting

If there is a problem, the partner selects a structured issue.

Examples:

- Out of stock.
- Package damaged.
- Wrong product.
- Missing component.
- Personalization unclear.
- Deadline impossible.
- Material unavailable.
- Courier delayed.
- Technical problem.

The partner should receive the next instruction.

Do not require the partner to solve policy questions alone.

---

## 23. Stage 19: Preparation

The preparation screen should show:

- Product checklist.
- Wrapping selection.
- Card text.
- Personalization.
- QR content.
- Deadline.
- Special handling.
- Evidence required.
- Customer-approved substitution if any.

The partner may mark:

- Started.
- Paused due to issue.
- Completed.

---

## 24. Stage 20: Evidence

The partner uploads evidence required by the order.

Possible evidence:

- Product received.
- Damage.
- Completed gift.
- Card.
- QR included.
- Packaging.
- Courier handoff.

The app should validate:

- File type.
- File size.
- Upload completion.
- Required evidence count.
- Order link.

---

## 25. Stage 21: Ready for pickup

Before marking ready, the partner confirms:

- Correct product.
- Correct personalization.
- Secure wrapping.
- Required evidence uploaded.
- Order label attached.
- Pickup area ready.

The order then becomes eligible for courier assignment or pickup.

---

## 26. Stage 22: Courier handoff

The partner should see:

- Courier or provider.
- Pickup time.
- Order reference.
- Delivery instructions.
- Identity verification method.

The partner records:

- Pickup timestamp.
- Courier identifier.
- Handoff evidence.
- Incident if courier does not arrive.

---

## 27. Stage 23: Delivery monitoring

The partner may view a limited delivery status:

- Courier assigned.
- Picked up.
- Out for delivery.
- Delivered.
- Delivery issue.
- Returned.

The partner should not contact the recipient unless instructed.

---

## 28. Stage 24: Completion

After delivery:

- Partner work is reviewed.
- Earnings remain pending or become approved according to rules.
- Incident responsibility is determined.
- The order moves toward completion.
- Partner metrics update.

The partner should see whether any action remains.

---

## 29. Stage 25: Earnings

The partner sees:

- Product earning.
- Reception earning.
- Inspection earning.
- Preparation earning.
- Wrapping earning.
- Personalization earning.
- Delivery earning.
- Bonus.
- Adjustment.
- Net amount.
- Status.

Earnings states:

```text
estimated
pending
approved
payable
paid
disputed
adjusted
```

---

## 30. Stage 26: Payout

The payout journey:

```text
earnings approved
→ included in payout period
→ payout prepared
→ payout sent
→ payment reference stored
→ partner notified
```

The partner sees:

- Period.
- Orders included.
- Total.
- Adjustments.
- Payment method.
- Status.
- Reference.
- Date.

---

## 31. Stage 27: Partner dispute

The partner may dispute:

- Missing earning.
- Wrong amount.
- Incorrect deduction.
- Incorrect incident responsibility.
- Missing payout.
- Incorrect order attribution.

Dispute flow:

```text
partner submits
→ MPHO reviews
→ evidence requested if needed
→ decision
→ adjustment when approved
→ closure
```

Original history must remain.

---

## 32. Stage 28: Performance review

The partner may view:

- Response time.
- Acceptance rate.
- Stock accuracy.
- Preparation time.
- On-time rate.
- Evidence quality.
- Incident rate.
- Customer feedback.
- Earnings trend.

Metrics should be explained and fair.

---

## 33. Stage 29: Improvement

Possible recommendations:

- Update stock more often.
- Improve evidence quality.
- Reduce response time.
- Add receiving capacity.
- Add premium wrapping.
- Expand operating hours.
- Complete training.
- Correct catalog information.

Recommendations should be actionable.

---

## 34. Stage 30: Temporary pause

The partner may pause due to:

- Vacation.
- Capacity.
- Staff shortage.
- Store closure.
- Inventory update.
- Technical issue.

Pause behavior:

- Stop new offers.
- Preserve active orders.
- Preserve history.
- Preserve earnings.
- Require completion or reassignment of existing work.

---

## 35. Stage 31: Restriction or suspension

MPHO may restrict or suspend because of:

- Repeated no-response.
- False stock.
- Quality failures.
- Data misuse.
- False evidence.
- Unsafe handling.
- Unauthorized substitution.
- Fraud.
- Repeated major incidents.

The partner should receive:

- Reason.
- Effective date.
- Impact.
- Required correction.
- Appeal or dispute path when applicable.

---

## 36. Stage 32: Offboarding

Offboarding flow:

```text
stop new offers
→ complete or reassign active orders
→ close incidents
→ finalize earnings
→ issue final payout
→ revoke access
→ remove public listings
→ preserve audit history
```

## 39. Partner error recovery

The app should support:

- Retry upload.
- Save draft evidence.
- Offline or low-connectivity recovery.
- Expired session recovery.
- Duplicate action protection.
- Clear state conflict message.
- Support contact.
- Manual exception creation.

---

## 40. Partner-journey success criteria

The journey succeeds when the partner:

- Understands the work.
- Can accept or reject quickly.
- Receives complete instructions.
- Can report problems.
- Can upload evidence.
- Can see deadlines.
- Can see earnings.
- Can understand payout.
- Can pause safely.
- Can resolve disputes.
- Does not need long manual coordination.

---

## 41. Summary

MPHO Aliados should act as a simple operational system.

A partner should always know:

1. What must be done?
2. By what time?
3. What evidence is required?
4. How much will be earned?
5. What happens next?
6. What to do if something fails?

The application must make partner work predictable, auditable, and financially transparent.
