## 2. Policy principles

- Make cancellation easy to request.
- Do not hide status.
- Distinguish request from approval.
- Show work already performed.
- Separate product, service, delivery, and external-purchase amounts.
- Do not refund more than paid.
- Do not refund twice.
- Do not automatically punish a partner for a provider failure.
- Preserve audit.
- Explain decisions.
- Provide escalation.
- Consider any statutory compensation or bonus that may apply.
- Use qualified legal review before publishing exclusions.

---

## 3. Definitions

```text
cancellation: request to stop performance
refund: return of money
partial refund: return of part of money
credit: non-cash benefit; not a substitute for refund unless freely accepted
dispute: disagreement about product, service, delivery, charge, or decision
chargeback: payment-provider or bank dispute
substitution: replacement of product or material element
reattempt: another delivery attempt
```

---

## 4. Order-stage matrix

The exact percentages or non-refundable amounts must be configured only after legal review.

| Stage | Typical operational effect | Potential customer outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Before payment | No transaction completed | Cancel without charge |
| Payment pending | Await provider truth | Stop new attempt; resolve provider state |
| Paid, no partner accepted | Minimal work | Full refund normally expected |
| Partner accepted, no materials used | Assignment work may exist | Full or near-full refund subject to lawful terms |
| Local stock reserved | Release stock | Refund subject to documented actual cost |
| External purchase not made | No external acquisition | Refund subject to lawful service work |
| External purchase completed | Third-party return terms apply | Refund depends on cancellation/return feasibility |
| Package in transit to partner | External shipping active | Hold decision until return path known |
| Package received and inspected | Services performed | Product and performed-service components separated |
| Personalization approved but not started | Reversible | Refund subject to actual work |
| Personalization started | Work/material may be irreversible | Partial or denied component only if lawful and disclosed |
| Gift fully prepared | Significant work performed | Review actual components and cause |
| Courier requested | Provider cancellation cost possible | Separate delivery cost |
| Courier picked up | Delivery in progress | Normal cancellation may no longer be feasible |
| Delivery failed | Cause analysis | Reattempt, return, partial/full refund depending cause |
| Delivered | Complaint or return rules | Product issue, warranty, quality, or statutory rights |
| Completed | Dispute still possible | Apply legal and policy deadlines |

Do not implement a universal “no refunds after payment” rule.

Cause affects internal allocation.

## 6. Refund calculation

Refund engine should calculate:

```text
amount_paid
minus lawful retained components
plus required correction or compensation
minus prior refunds
equals refundable balance
```

Components:

- Product.
- External shipping.
- Wrapping.
- Personalization.
- Preparation.
- MPHO service.
- Delivery.
- Tax.
- Discount.
- Statutory compensation where applicable.
- Goodwill adjustment.

The calculation must be reproducible.

---

## 7. Personalized products

Before irreversible work:

- Show exact design or text.
- Require approval.
- Record approval.
- Explain deadline.
- Explain possible effect on cancellation.
- Offer correction window when operationally possible.

A customer-approved spelling mistake may affect commercial remedy.

A mistake by MPHO or partner remains their responsibility.

No clause may eliminate mandatory rights for defective or incorrectly supplied work.

---

## 8. Perishable products

For flowers, food, fruit, cake, or other perishables:

- Explain storage and recipient-availability requirements.
- Explain failed-delivery consequences.
- Record condition.
- Avoid promising return where physical return is impossible.
- Provide remedies for damage, incorrect product, unsafe product, or nonconformity.
- Obtain category-specific legal review.

---

## 9. External products

When external purchase has occurred:

- Determine whether seller permits cancellation.
- Determine shipping status.
- Determine return address.
- Determine return cost.
- Determine refund destination.
- Preserve seller communication.
- Keep customer informed.
- Do not promise refund before external recovery unless MPHO chooses to bear that risk or law requires it.

Customer must not be charged for MPHO or partner error.

---

## 10. Delivery-related refunds

### Courier not assigned

Normally no performed delivery.

### Courier assigned but not moving

Provider cancellation terms may apply.

### Courier traveling to pickup

Actual provider charge may exist.

### Picked up

Delivery is in progress.

### Failed because of customer or recipient

Possible reattempt or return fee if:

- Clearly disclosed.
- Reasonable.
- Actually incurred.
- Not caused by MPHO, partner, or courier.
- Permitted by law.

### Failed because of MPHO, partner, or courier

The customer should not bear the failed service cost.

## 11. Product issue workflow

```text
report
→ acknowledge
→ collect reasonable evidence
→ classify issue
→ preserve product when appropriate
→ obtain partner/courier response
→ decide remedy
→ execute
→ notify
→ close
```

Issue types:

- Wrong product.
- Missing item.
- Damaged.
- Late.
- Personalization error.
- Quality concern.
- Counterfeit suspicion.
- Unsafe product.
- Failed delivery.
- Unauthorized substitution.
- Duplicate charge.

---

## 12. Evidence

Reasonable evidence may include:

- Photo.
- Video.
- Delivery record.
- Package condition.
- Chat.
- Product label.
- Partner evidence.
- Courier proof.
- Provider event.

Do not demand evidence that the customer cannot realistically produce.

Do not rely exclusively on a courier photo when the customer disputes it.

---

## 13. Deadlines

The final policy must define reasonable reporting and response times by category.

Do not use an unreasonably short deadline to eliminate legal rights.

Internal target examples may be used operationally, but public deadlines require legal review.

---

## 14. Refund execution

- Refund only to original payment method where provider and law allow.
- Never ask for card credentials by chat.
- Verify provider refund.
- Store provider reference.
- Notify customer.
- Explain that bank posting time depends on provider.
- Do not mark complete before confirmation.
- Correct invoice or accounting record as required.

---

## 15. Credits and coupons

A coupon or MPHO credit:

- Must not replace a required monetary refund without voluntary acceptance.
- Must state expiration.
- Must state restrictions.
- Must not conceal a cash-refund right.
- Must be recorded separately from money.

The MVP should avoid wallet-like stored value until legal review.

---

## 16. Chargebacks

If a chargeback occurs:

- Do not issue an additional duplicate refund.
- Freeze only affected financial allocation.
- Gather evidence.
- Respond through provider.
- Keep customer support open.
- Review partner payout.
- Record outcome.

A chargeback does not automatically prove fraud.

---

Levels:

Every decision should have:

## 18. Spanish customer policy skeleton

# Política de Cancelaciones, Reembolsos y Aclaraciones

## 18.1 Cómo solicitar

La persona usuaria puede solicitar ayuda desde el pedido o por:

```text
[SUPPORT_EMAIL]
[SUPPORT_PHONE_OR_WHATSAPP]
```

MPHO proporcionará un folio.

## 18.2 Antes del pago

Puede abandonar o cancelar la cotización sin cargo.

## 18.3 Después del pago

El resultado depende de la etapa real del pedido, el trabajo realizado, la compra externa, la personalización y la entrega.

MPHO mostrará:

- Estado.
- Componentes ejecutados.
- Importe.
- Opciones.
- Plazo estimado de resolución.

## 18.4 Cuando MPHO o un tercero responsable incumple

Cuando el producto, preparación o entrega no corresponda a lo ofrecido, MPHO revisará la corrección, reposición, reentrega, reembolso y cualquier compensación legal aplicable.

## 18.5 Personalización

Antes de iniciar trabajo irreversible se solicitará confirmación.

Los errores atribuibles a MPHO o al aliado no se consideran aprobados por la persona usuaria.

## 18.6 Entrega fallida

Se identificará la causa.

No se cobrará un reintento cuando la falla sea atribuible a MPHO, al aliado o al repartidor.

## 18.7 Reembolso

El reembolso aprobado se enviará al medio original cuando sea posible.

El tiempo de reflejo depende del proveedor y banco.

MPHO mostrará el estado y referencia disponible.

## 18.8 Derechos

Esta política no limita derechos irrenunciables de las personas consumidoras ni impide acudir a PROFECO u otra autoridad competente.

- Cancel before payment.
- Cancel paid unassigned order.
- External purchase already made.
- Personalization started.

- Failed delivery caused by courier.
- Wrong product.
