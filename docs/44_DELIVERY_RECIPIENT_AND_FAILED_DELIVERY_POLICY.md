## 2. Delivery principles

- Protect recipient privacy.
- Deliver only the correct order.
- Verify custody.
- Avoid false precision.
- Distinguish estimate from guarantee.
- Record failed attempt.
- Protect perishables.
- Do not leave a package in an unsafe place without authorization.
- Do not disclose surprise details unnecessarily.
- Do not make the customer pay for MPHO-caused failure.
- Preserve mandatory consumer rights.

---

## 3. Required delivery data

Collect only:

- Recipient name.
- Phone when needed.
- Address.
- map point.
- references.
- access instructions.
- delivery window.
- surprise mode.
- authorized alternative recipient.
- age verification flag where legally required.
- safety notes necessary for delivery.

Do not ask for unrelated personal details.

---

## 4. Address confirmation

Before payment:

- Show normalized address.
- Show map point.
- Ask customer to confirm.
- Ask for access reference.
- Validate zone.
- Validate delivery price.
- Warn when address appears incomplete.

After payment, address changes require:

- Risk check.
- Requote.
- provider approval when assigned.
- audit.
- reauthentication for high-risk change.
- confirmation of timing impact.

---

## 5. Delivery modes

```text
scheduled delivery
MPHORA fast delivery
partner own delivery
manual courier
integrated provider
future customer pickup
```

Each mode must show:

- Responsible delivery party.
- Price.
- estimated window.
- tracking level.
- proof.
- failed-delivery rules.

---

## 6. Surprise modes

### Full surprise

Do not reveal gift details unless required to complete delivery or protect safety.

### Discreet coordination

Tell the recipient that a delivery is expected without revealing content.

### Scheduled and disclosed

Recipient knows the delivery and may know the gift.

The customer must understand that a full surprise may fail if the recipient cannot be contacted or accessed.

---

## 7. Contact hierarchy

Suggested:

1. Recipient according to selected mode.
2. Customer.
3. Authorized alternate.
4. MPHO operations.
5. Return to partner.

The courier must not use recipient contact for personal or marketing purposes.

---

## 8. Courier verification

At pickup:

- Courier identity or provider reference.
- Order reference.
- package count.
- timestamp.
- partner confirmation.
- proof.

At delivery:

- Correct destination.
- recipient or authorized receiver.
- code/signature/photo when appropriate.
- timestamp.
- attempt reason if failed.

---

## 9. Proof of delivery

Possible proof:

- Provider confirmation.
- One-time code.
- Signature.
- Recipient name.
- Photo of package at authorized location.
- GPS and timestamp.
- Courier note.

Rules:

- Avoid photographing recipient unless necessary and permitted.
- Avoid showing house details beyond need.
- Protect proof as private data.
- Do not publish.
- Do not use proof for marketing.
- One proof method alone may not resolve every dispute.

---

## 10. Alternative recipient

The customer may authorize:

- Reception.
- Security desk.
- Family member.
- Neighbor.
- coworker.
- named person.

The system should record authorization.

For high-value or restricted categories, alternative delivery may be prohibited.

---

## 11. Safe-place delivery

The MVP should not allow unattended safe-place delivery by default.

If introduced:

- Explicit authorization.
- eligible product.
- low-risk location.
- photo.
- exact instructions.
- no perishables unless appropriate.
- no high-value item.
- no age-restricted item.
- risk disclosure.
- legal and insurance review.

## 13. Attempt procedure

On failed attempt:

1. Record time.
2. Record location or provider event.
3. Attempt approved contacts.
4. Wait only within the provider or policy limit.
5. Avoid unsafe waiting.
6. Record package condition.
7. Determine reattempt or return.
8. Notify customer.
9. Preserve proof.
10. Open incident when needed.

---

## 14. Reattempt

Before reattempt:

- Confirm package location.
- Confirm product condition.
- Confirm new time.
- Calculate actual cost.
- determine responsibility.
- obtain customer approval when the customer pays.
- update delivery record.
- assign courier.
- preserve original attempt.

No automatic reattempt charge.

---

## 15. Return to Punto MPHO

When returned:

- Partner verifies courier.
- Records receipt.
- Photographs condition when required.
- Stores securely.
- Notes perishable condition.
- Starts decision deadline.
- Notifies operations.
- Does not resell or use item.

---

## 16. Perishable or time-sensitive gift

For flowers, food, cake, or balloons:

- Define safe time.
- Define storage.
- Define reattempt feasibility.
- Define disposal approval.
- record condition.
- customer receives timely choices.
- do not promise the same quality after failed delivery.

Category-specific rules require legal review.

---

## 17. Recipient refusal

If recipient refuses:

- Do not pressure.
- Ask only necessary clarification.
- Do not disclose unnecessary buyer details.
- Record refusal.
- Contact customer.
- Return or hold.
- apply refund policy according to cause and product condition.

For safety or harassment concerns, stop contact and escalate.

---

## 18. Harassment and safety

MPHO must not be used to:

- Harass.
- threaten.
- stalk.
- repeatedly contact.
- send prohibited content.
- expose a recipient.
- bypass a block.

Controls:

- Recipient can refuse.
- support can block future contact.
- suspicious orders reviewed.
- law-enforcement requests handled formally.
- courier safety overrides delivery completion.
- gift message moderation for serious threats.

---

## 19. Restricted recipient situations

Additional review for:

- Hospital.
- school.
- prison.
- government facility.
- industrial facility.
- gated community.
- hotel.
- event venue.
- workplace with security.
- minor recipient.
- age-restricted product.
- high-value item.

Do not promise access.

---

## 20. Loss or theft

Flow:

```text
last custody confirmed
→ provider and partner contacted
→ evidence preserved
→ customer informed
→ replacement/refund review
→ insurance or provider claim
→ fraud review
```

Do not wait for provider reimbursement before giving the customer the remedy legally required.

---

## 21. Damage

If damage occurs:

- Stop delivery if unsafe.
- photograph.
- preserve package.
- classify source.
- contact customer.
- offer lawful remedy.
- record financial allocation.
- review partner/courier responsibility.

---

## 22. Force majeure and emergency

Potential events:

- Severe weather.
- road closure.
- civil emergency.
- provider outage.
- public-safety event.
- building evacuation.
- natural disaster.

The customer should receive:

- Verified facts.
- revised estimate.
- options.
- next update.

A force-majeure clause cannot be used to excuse ordinary preventable failures.

## 23. Spanish public skeleton

# Política de Entrega, Destinatarios y Entregas Fallidas

## 23.1 Ventanas de entrega

La fecha y ventana mostradas pueden ser solicitadas, estimadas o confirmadas.

MPHO identificará cuál corresponde.

Sólo se considerará garantía cuando se indique expresamente.

## 23.2 Datos del destinatario

Los datos se utilizarán para preparar, coordinar y entregar el pedido.

El destinatario no será agregado automáticamente a promociones.

## 23.3 Sorpresa

La persona compradora elegirá el nivel de sorpresa.

Si no es posible completar la entrega sin contacto, MPHO podrá comunicarse de forma discreta.

## 23.4 Intento de entrega

El repartidor deberá verificar el pedido y registrar el intento.

No dejará el regalo en un lugar inseguro sin autorización.

## 23.5 Entrega fallida

MPHO informará:

- Causa.
- Ubicación del regalo.
- Condición.
- Opciones.
- Costo de reintento cuando corresponda.
- Posible efecto en productos perecederos.

## 23.6 Responsabilidad del costo

No se cobrará a la persona compradora por un reintento cuando el fallo sea atribuible a MPHO, al Punto MPHO o al repartidor.

Cuando la causa sea una dirección incorrecta, ausencia o acceso no disponible, se informará cualquier costo real antes de solicitar autorización.

## 23.7 Rechazo

El destinatario puede rechazar el regalo.

MPHO no lo presionará ni revelará información innecesaria.

## 23.8 Privacidad

La evidencia de entrega se protege como información privada y no se usa para publicidad sin autorización.

## 23.9 Seguridad

MPHO puede detener una entrega cuando exista riesgo para el destinatario, repartidor, aliado o comunidad.
