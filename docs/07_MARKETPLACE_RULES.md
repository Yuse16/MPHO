# 07_MARKETPLACE_RULES.md

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the operating rules of the MPHO marketplace.

It establishes:

- Who may participate.
- What may be listed.
- How orders are accepted.
- How products are represented.
- How substitutions, cancellations, incidents, and disputes are handled.
- Which behaviors are prohibited.
- How trust and quality are protected.

These rules apply to customers, partners, operators, couriers, and external-product workflows.

---

## 2. Marketplace principles

MPHO must operate according to:

- Truthful availability.
- Clear pricing.
- Traceable orders.
- Defined responsibility.
- Fair partner treatment.
- Customer protection.
- Reliable evidence.
- Privacy.
- Controlled access.
- Human review for sensitive exceptions.

---

## 3. Participation rules

### Customers

A customer may:

- Browse.
- Purchase.
- Personalize.
- Provide recipient information.
- Track.
- Request support.
- Request cancellation according to policy.
- Submit feedback.

A customer must:

- Provide accurate information.
- Pay approved totals.
- Use lawful payment methods.
- Avoid prohibited products or messages.
- Respect delivery and cancellation conditions.

### Partners

A partner may participate only after review.

A partner must provide:

- Legal or business identity information required by MPHO.
- Contact information.
- Physical location.
- Operating schedule.
- Capabilities.
- Product and service information.
- Payment details.
- Agreement acceptance.
- Authorized staff.

### Couriers

A courier may participate directly or through a third-party service.

The courier must receive only the data required for delivery.

---

## 4. Partner approval

A business is not a Punto MPHO merely because it creates an account.

Approval may require:

- Identity review.
- Location review.
- Capability review.
- Product-category review.
- Packaging-quality review.
- Test order.
- Payment information.
- Signed agreement.
- Operating-hours confirmation.
- Safety and handling review.

Partner approval states may include:

```text
applicant
under_review
approved
restricted
paused
suspended
rejected
closed
```

---

## 5. Partner capabilities

A partner must be authorized for each capability.

Possible capabilities:

- Sell local products.
- Receive external packages.
- Inspect products.
- Store temporarily.
- Wrap.
- Assemble.
- Personalize.
- Capture evidence.
- Dispatch to courier.
- Deliver locally.

A partner must not perform unauthorized services.

---

## 6. Catalog publication rules

Every published listing must include accurate information.

Required fields may include:

- Name.
- Description.
- Images.
- Source type.
- Partner.
- Price.
- Availability.
- Preparation time.
- City.
- Zone.
- Variants.
- Add-ons.
- Personalization.
- Restrictions.
- Last verified date.
- Delivery classification.
- MPHORA eligibility when applicable.

MPHO may reject or pause listings that are:

- Misleading.
- Incomplete.
- Low quality.
- Duplicated.
- Prohibited.
- Incorrectly priced.
- Operationally unsupported.

---

## 7. Image rules

Product images must:

- Represent the real product or clearly marked reference.
- Have usage authorization.
- Avoid misleading size or quantity.
- Avoid unrelated branding.
- Avoid watermarks that imply unauthorized copying.
- Match available variants when possible.

For handmade or variable products, the listing must explain expected variation.

---

## 8. Local-product rules

A local product listing must identify:

- Which partner supplies it.
- Whether stock is tracked or confirmed on demand.
- Preparation time.
- Substitution policy.
- Product variation.
- Expiration of availability.

If stock is unknown, it must not be presented as confirmed.

---

## 9. External-product rules

An external product listing must show:

- That it is by order.
- Estimated acquisition or arrival time.
- External-price validation date.
- Potential variation.
- Preparation-point flow.
- Cancellation limitations.
- Return limitations.
- Delivery timing after receipt.

The customer must not be led to believe that MPHO physically owns an external product before purchase or receipt.

---

## 10. MPHORA rules

A product may appear in MPHORA only when:

- Stock is confirmed.
- Partner is active.
- Partner is open or available.
- Preparation capacity exists.
- Zone is active.
- Delivery can be arranged.
- Cutoff time has not passed.
- No unresolved blocking incident exists.

MPHORA eligibility may expire automatically.

---

## 11. Pricing rules

The customer must see the final total before payment.

The total may include:

- Product.
- Add-ons.
- Preparation.
- Personalization.
- Delivery.
- MPHO service.
- Taxes when applicable.
- Discount.

Prices must not change after payment unless:

- Customer approves a modification.
- A partial refund is issued.
- A documented substitution changes value.
- A legally required adjustment applies.

External-price changes before purchase must trigger review.

---

## 12. One responsible partner per order

In the MVP:

- One partner owns the operational responsibility.
- One partner receives external products.
- One partner prepares.
- One partner dispatches.

The partner may use an external courier, but the order must not be split across multiple preparation partners.

This rule simplifies:

- Responsibility.
- Evidence.
- Earnings.
- Customer communication.
- Exceptions.
- Quality.

---

## 13. Partner offer rules

An order offer must include:

- Order reference.
- Required product or service.
- Preparation requirements.
- Deadline.
- Delivery window.
- Expected earnings.
- Special instructions.
- Accept-by time.

The partner may:

- Accept.
- Reject.
- Report unavailable stock.
- Report capability mismatch.
- Request review when information is incomplete.

Silence does not count as acceptance.

---

## 14. Acceptance rules

Acceptance means the partner agrees to:

- Perform the listed work.
- Meet the stated timing.
- Follow preparation instructions.
- Capture required evidence.
- Report exceptions immediately.
- Protect recipient data.
- Hand off correctly.

The partner must not accept when it cannot reasonably fulfill.

---

## 15. Product reservation

For local products:

- Stock should be reserved after verified payment and partner acceptance.
- Reservation must reduce sellable availability.
- Reservation must expire or be released after cancellation.
- Overselling must create an exception.

---

## 16. External-package receipt

When receiving an external product, the partner must record:

- Date and time.
- Package condition.
- Product identity.
- Quantity.
- Visible damage.
- Photos when required.
- Missing components.
- Incorrect product.
- Receiver identity.

The partner must not mark `received` without actual physical receipt.

---

## 17. Inspection rules

Inspection is limited to authorized, safe, non-destructive review.

The partner may check:

- Product matches order.
- Visible condition.
- Quantity.
- Basic completeness.
- Obvious damage.

The partner must not:

- Break seals without authorization.
- Activate devices unnecessarily.
- Access private digital content.
- Use the product.
- Remove serial numbers.
- Modify the product before approval.

---

## 18. Preparation rules

The partner must follow:

- Selected wrapping.
- Selected message.
- Approved personalization.
- Product-handling instructions.
- Deadline.
- Evidence requirements.

If instructions are unclear, the partner must request clarification before irreversible work.

---

## 19. Substitution rules

A substitution is never automatic unless the customer pre-authorized a specific rule.

Possible substitution flow:

```text
item unavailable
→ partner reports issue
→ MPHO proposes approved alternatives
→ customer approves or rejects
→ price difference is resolved
→ order continues or cancels
```

A substitution record must include:

- Original item.
- Proposed replacement.
- Reason.
- Price difference.
- Customer approval.
- Timestamp.
- Actor.

---

## 20. Personalization rules

Personalized items may have special cancellation or refund conditions.

Before production begins, the customer must confirm:

- Text.
- Spelling.
- Color.
- Size.
- File.
- Message.
- Recipient name.
- QR destination when applicable.

The platform should preserve the approved personalization input.

---

## 21. Prohibited content

MPHO may reject:

- Illegal content.
- Threats.
- Harassment.
- Hate content.
- Fraud.
- Impersonation.
- Non-consensual intimate content.
- Instructions for harmful acts.
- Prohibited product requests.
- Messages that create safety concerns.

Human review may be required.

---

## 22. Restricted products

MPHO must maintain an approved and prohibited category policy.

Until specialized rules exist, avoid:

- Weapons.
- Hazardous materials.
- Controlled substances.
- Prescription medication.
- Products requiring licenses.
- Live animals.
- High-risk perishables.
- Age-restricted products.
- Products requiring cold chain.
- Extremely high-value items.
- Oversized items.
- Counterfeit products.

---

## 23. Delivery-address rules

The customer is responsible for providing accurate:

- Recipient name.
- Phone when required.
- Street.
- Number.
- Neighborhood.
- Postal code.
- City.
- References.
- Delivery instructions.

The platform should validate address completeness.

Address corrections after courier dispatch may create extra cost.

---

## 24. Recipient-contact rules

Recipient contact should be minimized when surprise is intended.

Possible modes:

- Full surprise.
- Contact only if delivery fails.
- Schedule directly with recipient.
- Customer handles coordination.

The selected mode must be clear to the courier and partner.

---

## 25. Delivery evidence

Delivery evidence may include:

- Timestamp.
- Courier identity.
- Recipient name or confirmation.
- Photo when appropriate.
- Signature or code.
- GPS or provider event when available.
- Incident note.

Evidence must respect privacy.

Do not require photos of people unless necessary and consented.

---

## 26. Failed delivery

Possible causes:

- Recipient absent.
- Incorrect address.
- Unsafe location.
- Access denied.
- Customer unreachable.
- Courier issue.
- Partner delay.
- Weather or external event.

Failed-delivery handling may include:

- Reattempt.
- Return to partner.
- New delivery fee.
- Rescheduling.
- Cancellation.
- Partial refund.
- No refund for consumed preparation work.

The responsible cause must be recorded.

---

## 27. Cancellation rules

Cancellation eligibility depends on order stage.

Possible stages:

### Before payment

No financial effect.

### After payment but before partner acceptance

Usually eligible for cancellation, subject to processing conditions.

### After partner acceptance but before preparation

May incur committed costs.

### After personalization starts

May be partially or fully non-refundable.

### After external purchase

Depends on external provider rules and shipping status.

### After courier pickup

Generally not cancellable as a normal cancellation.

Final policy must be defined in later documents.

---

## 28. Refund rules

Refunds must be:

- Authorized.
- Recorded.
- Linked to a reason.
- Linked to the original payment.
- Reflected in partner earnings.
- Reflected in MPHO revenue.
- Reflected in order status.

A customer-support promise does not count as a completed refund.

---

## 29. Partner earnings disputes

A partner may dispute:

- Missing earning line.
- Incorrect amount.
- Incorrect incident responsibility.
- Incorrect deduction.
- Missing payout.
- Wrong order attribution.

The dispute must include:

- Order reference.
- Reason.
- Evidence.
- Requested correction.

The system must preserve the original financial record and add an adjustment rather than silently rewriting history.

---

## 30. Customer disputes

A customer may report:

- Product mismatch.
- Damage.
- Late delivery.
- Missing item.
- Incorrect personalization.
- Failed delivery.
- Unexpected charge.
- Missing refund.

Each dispute should become an incident with:

- Category.
- Evidence.
- Responsible actor.
- Resolution.
- Financial effect.
- Timeline.

---

## 31. Quality rules

MPHO may define:

- Packaging standards.
- Photo standards.
- Response-time expectations.
- Preparation checklists.
- Handoff requirements.
- Maximum incident rates.
- Training requirements.

Repeated quality failures may lead to:

- Warning.
- Reduced order priority.
- Temporary pause.
- Capability restriction.
- Suspension.
- Removal.

---

## 32. Partner conduct

Partners must not:

- Contact customers for unauthorized sales.
- Move the transaction outside MPHO.
- Replace items without approval.
- Add unauthorized promotional material.
- Share customer or recipient data.
- Delay status updates intentionally.
- Claim false completion.
- Manipulate evidence.
- Charge the customer separately.
- Use external products before delivery.

---

## 33. Customer conduct

Customers must not:

- Use stolen payment methods.
- Submit false disputes.
- Request prohibited items.
- Abuse partners or couriers.
- Use MPHO to harass recipients.
- Provide intentionally false addresses.
- Request unsafe delivery.
- Attempt platform circumvention.

---

## 34. Courier conduct

Couriers must not:

- Open the gift.
- Alter packaging.
- Share recipient information.
- Mark delivered without evidence.
- Charge the recipient unless explicitly authorized.
- Keep the package after a failed attempt without instructions.

---

## 35. Data and privacy rules

Participants may access only necessary data.

Partner access may include:

- Order contents.
- Preparation instructions.
- Recipient delivery data when required.
- Courier handoff data.

Partner access must not include unrelated customer history or other partners' data.

---

## 36. Marketplace enforcement

Potential enforcement actions:

```text
notice
warning
temporary_pause
listing_removal
capability_restriction
financial_hold
suspension
termination
```

Enforcement must be:

- Documented.
- Proportionate.
- Reviewable.
- Linked to evidence.

---

## 37. Auditability

Critical actions must record:

- Actor.
- Timestamp.
- Previous state.
- New state.
- Reason.
- Evidence.
- Financial impact.

This includes:

- Acceptance.
- Rejection.
- Receipt.
- Damage report.
- Substitution.
- Preparation.
- Handoff.
- Delivery.
- Cancellation.
- Refund.
- Earnings adjustment.

---

## 38. Marketplace summary

MPHO is a controlled marketplace, not an unrestricted listing platform.

The marketplace must protect:

- Accurate product information.
- Real availability.
- Customer trust.
- Partner fairness.
- Delivery traceability.
- Financial integrity.
- Privacy.
- Operational accountability.

Every order must have clear responsibility from payment through delivery.
