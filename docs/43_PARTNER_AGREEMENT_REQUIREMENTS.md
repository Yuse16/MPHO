# 43_PARTNER_AGREEMENT_REQUIREMENTS.md

## 1. Status

```text
INTERNAL PARTNER-CONTRACT REQUIREMENTS
NOT A SIGNABLE AGREEMENT
LEGAL, TAX, LABOR, PRIVACY, AND INSURANCE REVIEW REQUIRED
```

This document defines what the final Punto MPHO agreement must address.

The actual agreement may differ by partner type:

```text
product seller
package receiving point
preparation point
personalization provider
own-delivery partner
multi-capability partner
```

---

## 2. Contract parties

Complete:

- MPHO legal entity.
- Partner legal entity or individual.
- RFC.
- Legal address.
- Representative.
- Public business name.
- Operating location.
- Payment beneficiary.
- Notices.
- Agreement effective date.
- Version.
- Attached capability schedule.

---

## 3. Relationship classification

The agreement must accurately describe the relationship.

It should not rely only on a label such as “independent contractor.”

Counsel must review actual facts:

- Control.
- Schedule.
- Exclusivity.
- Tools.
- Supervision.
- Staff.
- Economic dependence.
- Payment.
- Discipline.
- Integration into MPHO.

The system must not implement controls that contradict the approved model.

---

## 4. Scope of services

The capability schedule should specify whether the partner may:

- Sell local products.
- Confirm stock.
- Receive external packages.
- Inspect.
- Store.
- Wrap.
- Assemble.
- Personalize.
- Print.
- Create QR media.
- Handoff to courier.
- Deliver.
- Handle returns.
- Dispose of damaged or perishable items.

No capability is implied.

---

## 5. Partner onboarding representations

Partner should represent, subject to counsel:

- It exists and may contract.
- It controls the operating location.
- Information is accurate.
- Required permits exist.
- Staff is authorized.
- Products are lawful.
- Products are authentic.
- It owns or may license catalog content.
- Payout account is authorized.
- It will protect customer and recipient data.
- It will notify changes.
- It is not using MPHO for fraud.

---

## 6. Catalog obligations

Partner must:

- Use accurate title and description.
- Provide real photos or authorized representative images.
- Maintain price.
- Maintain availability.
- Identify variants.
- Identify relevant ingredients, restrictions, and handling.
- Avoid counterfeit goods.
- Avoid prohibited goods.
- Avoid misleading “local,” “handmade,” “official,” or “original” claims.
- Update discontinued products.
- Respect published price for accepted order.

MPHO retains catalog moderation rights.

---

## 7. Stock obligations

For stock-managed listings:

- Record on-hand quantity.
- Record reservations.
- Report damage.
- Confirm stale stock.
- Perform counts.
- Avoid accepting unavailable stock.
- Preserve inventory evidence when requested.
- Report incident immediately.

Repeated stock inaccuracy may restrict MPHORA or suspend listing.

---

## 8. Package receipt and custody

For external packages:

- Use assigned order.
- Verify package reference.
- Record receipt.
- Record visible condition.
- Photograph according to policy.
- Store securely.
- Separate orders.
- Limit staff access.
- Report mismatch.
- Not open beyond authorized inspection.
- Not use, sell, replace, or remove contents.
- Follow return instructions.
- Preserve chain of custody.

The agreement must define when custody starts and ends.

---

## 9. Inspection

Inspection may include:

- Package condition.
- Product identity.
- Visible damage.
- Quantity.
- Variant.
- Basic compatibility with order.

Inspection does not automatically certify:

- Authenticity.
- Hidden defect.
- Regulatory compliance.
- Safety.
- Full functionality.

Do not market inspection beyond its real scope.

---

## 10. Preparation and personalization

Partner must:

- Follow order instructions.
- Verify approved text.
- Use agreed materials.
- Avoid unauthorized substitutions.
- Keep product secure.
- Meet timing.
- Upload required evidence.
- Mark ready only when complete.
- Report error before handoff.
- Avoid adding partner advertising unless approved.

---

## 11. Evidence

Evidence requirements may include:

- Timestamp.
- Order identifier.
- Package.
- Product.
- Preparation.
- Personalization.
- Handoff.
- Return condition.

Partner must not:

- Reuse evidence.
- Edit evidence deceptively.
- Upload another customer's content.
- expose recipient data publicly.
- retain private media beyond permitted use.

MPHO may perform random audits.

---

## 12. Delivery and handoff

Partner must:

- Verify courier.
- Verify order.
- Record package count.
- Record handoff.
- Avoid giving gift to unauthorized person.
- Follow surprise mode.
- Report courier delay.
- Receive returned package.
- Preserve condition.

Own-delivery partners need additional schedule covering:

- Driver.
- Vehicle.
- insurance.
- privacy.
- proof.
- failed delivery.
- cash prohibition in MVP.
- safety.

---

## 13. Data protection

Partner receives data only for assigned work.

Required obligations:

- Confidentiality.
- Purpose limitation.
- Least access.
- Staff training.
- Device security.
- No screenshots unless required.
- No contact outside order purpose.
- No marketing to recipient.
- No copying customer list.
- No exporting.
- No sharing.
- Incident notification.
- Deletion or return.
- Support for data-right requests.
- Security controls.
- Subcontractor restrictions.

The agreement must classify the partner's data role for each processing activity.

---

## 14. Security

Partner must:

- Use named accounts.
- Use MFA where required.
- Protect phone and device.
- Remove former employees.
- Report account takeover.
- Avoid shared password.
- Avoid unofficial links.
- Use MPHO for official status.
- Not request payment outside approved flow.
- Not change payout account through support chat.
- Not connect unapproved automation.
- Cooperate with incident response.

---

## 15. Customer contact and non-circumvention

Partner may contact customer or recipient only:

- Through approved channel.
- For assigned order.
- With necessary information.
- During the allowed period.

The agreement may prohibit deliberate circumvention, such as:

- Moving an active MPHO order outside platform.
- Requesting direct payment.
- Using customer data for unrelated sales.
- Copying recipient list.

The restriction must be reasonable and legally reviewed.

---

## 16. Earnings

The agreement must attach a current earning schedule.

It should define:

- Product earning.
- Reception.
- Inspection.
- Storage.
- Wrapping.
- Personalization.
- Handoff.
- Own delivery.
- Bonus.
- Adjustment.
- Currency.
- Tax.
- Invoice or receipt requirements.
- Payable milestone.
- Payout frequency.
- Minimum payout.
- Dispute process.
- Chargeback effect.
- Cancellation compensation.

Do not reserve a broad right to change completed-order earnings retroactively.

---

## 17. Payout account

- Partner must control the account.
- Change requires strong verification.
- Cooling period.
- Previous-contact notification.
- Audit.
- Hold for suspicious change.
- Partner responsible for accurate data, subject to MPHO's verification duties.
- No payout to a new destination before approval.

---

## 18. Taxes and invoicing

The agreement must state, after accountant review:

- Who issues invoice.
- To whom.
- For what amount.
- Tax treatment.
- Withholding.
- Required documents.
- Deadline.
- Effect of refund.
- Effect of adjustment.
- Partner responsibility for own taxes.

Do not use generic tax language without confirming the operating model.

---

## 19. Quality and service levels

Define measurable expectations:

- Offer response.
- Stock accuracy.
- Receipt timing.
- Preparation time.
- evidence.
- packaging.
- handoff.
- incident response.
- customer complaint cooperation.

A service level must have:

```text
metric
target
measurement
exception
consequence
review
```

Do not promise customer guarantee solely from an internal partner target.

---

## 20. Materials and equipment

Define ownership and payment for:

- Wrapping.
- Boxes.
- Labels.
- printers.
- QR materials.
- storage.
- camera/device.
- signage.
- uniforms.
- delivery materials.

Define what happens at termination.

---

## 21. Insurance and liability

Counsel must determine appropriate insurance for:

- Premises.
- Product.
- package custody.
- theft.
- fire.
- water damage.
- employee action.
- delivery.
- vehicle.
- cyber/privacy.
- high-value items.

The agreement should allocate liability based on control and fault without attempting to eliminate non-waivable obligations.

---

## 22. Incident duties

Partner must report:

- Missing package.
- Damaged package.
- Wrong product.
- counterfeit suspicion.
- unsafe product.
- data exposure.
- account compromise.
- courier issue.
- customer conflict.
- law-enforcement request.
- injury.
- loss.
- theft.
- fire.
- delayed preparation.

Define immediate channel and maximum reporting time.

---

## 23. Audit and verification

MPHO may review, subject to reasonable scope:

- Capability.
- location.
- inventory.
- order evidence.
- security.
- data protection.
- service quality.
- payout records.
- incident records.

Audits must respect partner confidential information and legal boundaries.

---

## 24. Suspension

Possible grounds:

- Security incident.
- fraud.
- counterfeit.
- repeated stock error.
- package loss.
- privacy violation.
- abusive conduct.
- unauthorized payment request.
- lack of permits.
- material misrepresentation.
- dangerous operation.
- refusal to cooperate with active incident.

Suspension should define:

- Scope.
- Existing orders.
- Data access.
- inventory.
- packages.
- payouts.
- investigation.
- appeal.
- termination.

---

## 25. Termination

Define:

- Ordinary notice.
- Immediate termination.
- Active-order completion.
- package return.
- customer data deletion.
- brand removal.
- final payout.
- disputes.
- survival clauses.
- audit and evidence.
- access revocation.

Partner access should end promptly.

---

## 26. Changes

Material changes to:

- Earnings.
- payout.
- data.
- scope.
- liability.
- service levels.
- brand.
- termination.

Should require notice and, when appropriate, affirmative acceptance.

Completed-order economics should remain governed by the accepted version.

---

## 27. Dispute process

Use:

- Operational review.
- Evidence.
- Written decision.
- Escalation.
- Mediation or negotiation when appropriate.
- Applicable law and venue reviewed by counsel.

Do not make dispute resolution impractical for a small local partner.

## 29. Product requirements

MPHO Aliados must:

- Display current agreement version.
- Store acceptance.
- Store capability schedule.
- Enforce role.
- show earning before acceptance.
- show deadline.
- prevent unapproved capability.
- show data notice.
- record evidence.
- provide incident button.
- support account revocation.
- preserve final payout statement.
